Diagram Display

Use the buttons below to display a selection of sentence diagrams in the display space. Pay particular attention to the primitives that are used to diagram the different grammatical components of each sentence. A breakdown of the sentences that can be displayed can be found below the display space. Also visit the about page to find out more about the different primitives that are used in the diagrams displayed.

Diagram Descriptions

Below is a description of each of the diagrams that you can visualise in the display space. Visit the about page to learn about the colour-coded parts of speech used below.

Basic sentence

An example of how a basic sentence containing only a subject and a verb can be represented by a sentence diagram.

Sentence:

Tom shouted.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(Tom), verb(shouted).

Associated tags: NN, V.

Question

An example of how a question can be represented as a sentence diagram.


Sentence:

Why are you laughing?

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(you), verb(are laughing), adverb(why).

Associated tags: NN, V, RB.

Passive verb

An example of how a sentence containing a passive verb can be represented by a sentence diagram.

Sentence:

The sun is shining.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(sun), verb(is shining), determiner(The).

Associated tags: NN, V, DT.

Predicate noun

An example of how a sentence containing a predicate noun can be represented by a sentence diagram.

Sentence:

Blue is a colour.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(Blue), verb(is), determiner(a), predicate noun(colour).

Associated tags: NN, V, DT.


Adverb

An example of how a sentence containing an adverb can be represented by a sentence diagram.

Sentence:

He ran very slowly.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(He), verb(ran), adverb(very,slowly).

Associated tags: NN, V, RB.

Adjective

An example of how a sentence containing an adjective can be represented by a sentence diagram

Sentence:

The yellow ducks quacked.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(ducks), verb(quacked), adjective(yellow), determiner(The).

Associated tags: NN, V, JJ, DT.

Prepositional Phrase

An example of how a sentence containing a prepositional phrase can be represented by a sentence diagram.

Sentence:

The horse galloped through the empty field.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(horse, field), verb(galloped), determiner(the), adjective(empty), preposition(through).

Associated tags: NN, V, JJ, DT, PP.

An example of how a sentence containing a direct object can be represented by a sentence diagram.

Sentence:

David drove home.

Sentence breakdown:

Parts of speech: noun(David), verb(drove), adverb(home).

Associated tags: NN, V, RB.
