Similarity

Sentence diagrams and syntax trees are both methods of pictorially representing the grammatical structure of a sentence. Both being tree structures that express a sentence in terms of its grammatical structure, makes the two very similar. Both demonstrate the grammatical modifiers of a sentence extending from the subject and verb parents. Where the two differ is how the grammatical components are represented within the tree diagram. Syntax trees represent grammatical components as decedents of one another using lines to connect each component. Sentence diagrams go a step further in that they demonstrate how words relate to each other by using various different drawing primitives to represent each component. Below is an example of a sentence represented as a parse tree, a syntax tree and a sentence diagram. Notice how similar the syntax tree and sentence diagram are.
